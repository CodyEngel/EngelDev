Last night I was bored on YouTube and decided to search for tech company office tours. As it turns out, TechCrunch has an entire series of videos accurately named "TC Cribs". These videos are in the same format of MTV Cribs, which I assume is no longer on the air, but it was my favorite show to watch in middle school. I've always been fascinated by the interior design of others, and since I've entered the grown up world, that fascination has shifted to office spaces. Currently I work in a large corporate office, the building hasn't received much TLC, so I find myself jealous of people working for companies with such amazing office spaces.
Here's a short list of my favorite office tours from the TC Cribs series.

## Credit Karma's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oJOzFeetXIE

Credit Karma is one of the tech unicorns out there, located in the historic Phelan building. Operating in weekly product iterations, they have been growing like crazy, hiring over 250 new employees since 2013. Their office is newly renovated, and while they are a financial company they try to keep things fun and less corporate feeling. Software engineers can expect to earn between $88,000 - $172,000 per year and are offered a 100% match on their 401k plan.

## DogVacay's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HAOxdZE9iHk

DogVacay is located in Culver City, California. The entire office was designed with dogs in mind; on any given day their office has 30–50 dogs and a full time dog walker. On Friday's the company caters dog donuts, they look like real donuts but aren't intended for people. If you want to bring your dog to work, this is probably the best place to work.

## Lyft's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F1M-OSR7Zw

Lyft is located in San Francisco's Mission District, setup with an open floor plan and plenty of common areas to help keep employees away from their desks. The 66,000 square foot office has an elevator lined with fuzzy pink fur which is carried throughout the rest of the office. They even have a secret Willy Wonka room filled with alcohol and men with moustaches. If you are in the market for a new job, software engineers can expect to make between $117,000 and $162,000 per year; not too bad.

## Trulia's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DqQGVGCyodc

Trulia is located next to the Transbay Terminal and features a nature-inspired design. The desks are placed like tree rings, and it's the most energy efficient building currently in San Francisco. The company features unlimited time off and a very open work environment where employees are free to work from home whenever they need to. The philosophy at the company is to treat employees like adults and they'll act like it, they even have a "no assholes" policy. Engineers can expect to earn anywhere between $107,000 and $192,000 per year.

## Thumbtack's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U3W_llK61sA

Thumbtack's office has a very industrial feel and was originally a furniture shop with rumors of having a grow house on the 3rd and 4th floors. So many employees bike in that their office is sometimes confused for a bike shop. The company has five on-staff chefs who prepare lunch and dinner every day, they also feature an unlimited vacation policy which seems to be the norm at most startups these days.

## Slack's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K3tuMJVM3Ao

Slack is headquartered on 5th Street in San Francisco just a couple blocks south of Market Street, and shares a building with Eventbrite. Employees are grouped by the noise level produced, which essentially ends up with engineers on one side and sales people on another. Their office is 50,000 square feet with 30 conference room and a massive kitchen area. Salary information is sparse but an engineer can expect to make anywhere from $125,000 - $196,000.

## Glassdoor's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4H-GByrg1WQ

Glassdoor is located in Sausalito which is just 10 miles away from downtown San Francisco. Glassdoor employs 400+ employees and provide them with a massive game room, their own band, and a full bar. Everyday they also bring in free food for all employees, and a gym to work off those extra calories. If the gym isn't your thing, they also have a lake with paddle boarding and plenty of walking paths. An engineer working at Glassdoor can expect to make between $80,000 and $178,000 per year.

## Yelp's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bShTkczeXT0

Yelp just celebrated its 10th birthday, and late last summer moved into a gorgeous new 12-story office space in San Francisco's historic PacBell building. The office features a coffee shop in a large lounge area where employees can come to hang out or host meetings. Engineers at Yelp can expect to earn anywhere between $88,000 and and $154,000 a year.

## Twitch's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dvA9uZdFYAg

Twitch is often called the ESPN of online gaming. It's one of the hottest companies around these days. So it was no surprise to see that Twitch's San Francisco headquarters is basically a big shrine to all things gaming, with big and small references to everything from games like Super Mario, to Portal, to Halo, and everything in between. Their office features a LAN room with 10 networked gaming computers along with all of the live streaming feeds you could ever want. An engineer can make anywhere between $89,000 and up to $183,000 per year.

## Pinterest's Office Tour

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cXPDhwBAMpA

From the outside, Pinterest is known for being one of the Internet's go-to places for beautiful things. But on the inside, the company is also killing it from an engineering perspective - and tech folks have sometimes been known to neglect decorating their own work spaces to focus on making great products for their customers. If you're in the market for a new job, engineers make anywhere from $102,000 - $165,000 per year. Their office also have make-a-thons which are like hack-a-thons, except you can also build non-tech stuff too. Oh, and they have catered lunch.
